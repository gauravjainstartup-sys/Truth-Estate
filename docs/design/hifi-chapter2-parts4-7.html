The cases that matter to an Arbour buyer
DLF v. Capital Greens Flat Buyers Assn.
Supreme Court of India · 14 Dec 2020
DisposedContextualImpact: High

The Supreme Court upheld an NCDRC order directing DLF to pay 6% p.a. compensation for unjustified possession delay in Gurugram. DLF's force-majeure defence was rejected; hundreds of buyers were compensated.

What it means for you
A confirmed, top-court loss for possession delay — same city, same type of project. Delivery risk here is documented, not hypothetical.
SC judgment · Civil Appeal Nos. 3864–3889/2020 · view source ↗
Belaire Owners' Assn. v. DLF — CCI Penalty
CCI → Appellate Tribunal → Supreme Court
DisposedIndirectImpact: High

CCI imposed a ₹630 crore penalty on DLF for abusing a dominant position through unfair, one-sided buyer agreements in Belaire & Park Place. The competition finding was upheld through appellate stages.

What it means for you
DLF's standard buyer contracts have been judicially found one-sided at the highest levels. Have a lawyer read The Arbour's Agreement to Sell before you sign.
CCI order 2011 · SC deposit direction Aug 2014 · view source ↗
⚑ Before you sign — your due-diligence plan
  1. Verify HRERA status & latest QPR yourself on haryanarera.gov.in using RERA No. GGM/671/403/2023/15.
  2. Get an independent lawyer to read the Agreement to Sell clause-by-clause — penalty terms, force-majeure wording, and delay-compensation are where DLF has lost before.
  3. Insist the revised RERA possession date is written into the builder-buyer agreement, and hold ~5% against on-time handover.
